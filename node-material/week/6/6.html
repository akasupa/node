Week 6 - Production

It's not enough to just develop node.js applications, but you will also need to deploy them, often to multiple environments like development, testing, staging and production. While you finish up your Group Project, we'll be covering the the requirements of deploying production-quality node.js applications.

Group Milestone - Demo Day

Each team will showcase the project they have built and give a tour to the class in a brief presentation to review the work we have done over the course of the class. We'll have food and drinks, and we'll celebrate your first Node.js application!

This week we will be focusing on refining your application. Make sure to benchmark real apps and start skinning your interface to improve the look and feel in addition to focusing on performance and stability. Continue polishing your application and refining APIs.

Deliverable: (Before June 4th) Teams will be presenting their app at our internal demo day.

Sprint Goal: The focus of this final abbreviated sprint is to polish your app and improve the API and interface to adhere to proper design patterns for Node.js and REST APIs. This week your group should:

  • Break down work for each team member to complete for this week into issues on Github
  • Review Node.js Design Guidelines
  • Compare with apps on sites like NodeKnockout and with REST APIs like Dropbox, Twitter and Stripe
  • Skin your app's appearance using front-end frameworks like Bootstrap or Skeleton.
  • Bring your app to life by adding PubSub/push with socket.io, PubNub or Pusher.

Once your sprint is complete (before June 4th):

  • Please include an updated GIF in the README to record the latest improvements to your app
  • Useful to compare your sprint 2 GIF and sprint 3 GIF to measure improvement to your UI and functionality.
